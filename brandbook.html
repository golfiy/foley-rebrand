01 · Essence

What Foley means now.

The brand is moving from Foley Services / Carrier Services to simply Foley. A platform, not a service. Data and AI, not paperwork. The wordmark — three concentric blue rings with a green arc — stays. We just give it a calmer, more modern environment to live in.

Audience
Safety and HR directors in transportation, retail, construction, agriculture, healthcare. Bought by COO and CFO.
02 · Logo

The mark stays. The frame changes.

Foley's existing wordmark — three concentric rings with a green arc forming the "O" — is the brand asset. Use it as the canonical FOLEY mark; do not redraw, recolor, or replace it. Two base modes: full color on light surfaces, white wordmark on dark surfaces.

Do

Use the supplied PNG / SVG. The symbol mark (rings only) is for favicons, app icons, and slide footers when space is tight.

Do

Minimum clearspace = the height of the lowercase "o" around the wordmark. Minimum size on a slide: 24 px tall.

Don't

Don't redraw, recolor, or change the proportions of the rings. Don't use "Foley Services" or "Foley Carrier Services" — those names are discontinued.

Don't

Don't place the logo on busy photos without a backing surface. Don't tint it, drop‑shadow it, or animate the rings.

03 · Color

Their blue, fresher.

The palette respects the existing logo blues but updates them for screen and modern executive contexts. Foley Navy replaces the old corporate dark blue with a deeper, cooler tone. Foley Blue takes the bright cyan from the logo and pushes it slightly cleaner. Green is retained — but only as the semantic success state, not a brand accent.

05 · Imagery

Honest documentary, not stock cliché.

Three modes: Navy (dark brand surface with subtle dot grid and a blue graph line), Blue (for hero moments and stat slides), and Mist (for analytics and UI screenshots on light). Photography means real drivers, depots, dispatch rooms — never staged handshakes.

Navy · UI · charts
Blue · KPI · stat moments
Mist · analytics · UI shots
+ Do
  • Real people in context: cab, depot, office
  • UI screenshots on a Navy backing surface
  • Clean dot grids and thin line charts
  • Green only as a status indicator: "cleared", "passed"
  • Generous whitespace, generous margins
× Don't
  • Yellow‑orange gradients, retro hues
  • Stock handshakes and staged office shots
  • Heavy drop shadows, embossed effects
  • Cartoon clipart icons, multicolor flat illustrations
  • ALL CAPS headlines everywhere — keep them for eyebrows / labels only

08 · Voice & tone

Calm, confident, never preachy.

Foley knows the regulations, but doesn't lecture about them. It sounds like a senior colleague who just spotted a problem before it became a fine — and is calmly telling you what to do about it.

Foley sounds like
  • Hire in days, not weeks.
  • Get your fleet to clear — and keep it there.
  • Foley surfaces the signals. Navigator explains what they mean.
  • From 5 drivers to 500, one platform.
Foley does NOT sound like
  • Revolutionary, world‑class, best‑in‑breed solutions.
  • Leveraging synergies to drive compliance excellence.
  • YOUR #1 PARTNER FOR DOT REGULATIONS!!!
  • Click here to learn more about our amazing services.
